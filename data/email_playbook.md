# COLD EMAIL PLAYBOOK — Universal Guidelines

This playbook defines the universal standards for all outgoing cold emails generated by the Job Scout Engine.

---

## Section 1: Universal Rules (STRICT)

These rules apply to every email, regardless of the target company, role, or poster archetype.

### Formatting & Syntax
*   **NO DASHES FOR PUNCTUATION**: Em dashes (—), en dashes (–), and hyphens (-) must never be used for punctuation or pauses. Use commas or periods instead. Hyphens are only permitted inside URLs or email addresses. Write "systems first" instead of "systems-first", and "AI native" instead of "AI-native".
*   **BANNED WORDS**: Absolutely no filler words like "moreover", "furthermore", "thus", "therefore", or "herein".
*   **NO RESUME REFERENCES**: Do not write "Please find attached my resume" or "refer to my resume". The resume is attached programmatically.
*   **NO AI FLUFF**: Opener lines like "I hope this email finds you well" or closing filler are strictly prohibited.

### Intro & Identity
*   **Intro Sentence**: The second sentence of the email body must introduce the candidate exactly as:
    `I'm [Your Name], an AI native growth operator with 4+ years of experience, having managed 24+ brands.`
*   **Dynamic Title**: The signature block must match the specific domain of the role:
    *   Marketing/Paid Media: `AI Native Growth Operator`
    *   Product/Builder: `Growth & Product Operator`
    *   Automation/Systems: `Growth Systems Architect`
*   **Portfolio Link**: The portfolio URL (`https://shikhar-portfolio-marketing.vercel.app`) must be woven naturally into the body paragraphs. Do not place it in a separate line or footnote.

### Flow & Tone
*   **Sign-off**: Always close the body text with exactly:
    `Looking forward to talking to you guys.`
*   **Short Sentences**: Keep sentences short and punchy. Vary sentence lengths to create rhythm.

---

## Section 2: Strategy Archetypes Summary

The engine adapts to the post style by referencing these strategic archetypes:
1.  **Builder**: For founders requesting builders without formal JDs. Proposes concrete project solutions instead of standard marketing pitches.
2.  **Operator**: For structured JDs and HR contacts. Focuses on matching key skills and performance metrics.
3.  **Bridge**: For cross industry applications. Acknowledges the industry gap and bridges it by highlighting universal growth economics.
4.  **Challenger**: For founders facing specific growth bottlenecks. Offers hyper specific observations instead of self marketing.
5.  **Urgent**: For immediate openings. Ultra short (under 150 words) focusing on availability and speed to deploy.
6.  **Contrarian**: For casual posts rejecting resumes. Uses unconventional story hooks to establish peer level connections.

---

## Section 3: Prohibited Patterns (Bad vs. Good)

### 1. Punctuation Pauses
*   **BAD**: I am a systems-first marketer—focusing on paid acquisition—with 4 years of experience.
*   **GOOD**: I am a systems first marketer, focusing on paid acquisition, with 4 years of experience.

### 2. Resume Referencing
*   **BAD**: Please review my attached resume for more information about my metrics.
*   **GOOD**: I have managed over 20 brands, delivering up to 13x return on ad spend.

### 3. Filler Openings
*   **BAD**: Dear Himanshu, I hope this email finds you well. I am writing to express my interest...
*   **GOOD**: Hi Himanshu, I saw your post looking for AI Product Builders to join Stylework.
