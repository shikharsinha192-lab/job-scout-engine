## Email Rules (STRICT)

### DO:
- **Greeting:** Start with "Hey [First name]," or "Hi [First name],". (If name is unknown, use "Hi Hiring Team,")
- **Line 1 (Source):** Explicitly state where you got the job post from in the first sentence.
- **Line 2 (Intro):** Introduce yourself EXACTLY with the core stats intro sentence.
- **Storytelling & Flow:** Make the emails slightly longer and weave a narrative. Do not just list disconnected metrics. Connect your experience to the specific problem their company is solving. 
- **Tone & Rhythm:** Keep it sharp, but use varied sentence lengths to build a narrative. 
- **Portfolio:** Naturally integrate the portfolio link directly into the body text of the email.
- **Pointers/Bullets:** Keep pointers to an absolute maximum of 3, and only use them if strictly needed.
- **Action Note:** End the body with an actionable note (e.g., "Let's schedule a call soon.").
- **Final Sign-off (STRICT):** 
  Looking forward to talking to you guys.

  Thanks and regards,

### Prohibited Elements:
   - NO em dashes (—) or en dashes (–) or regular hyphens (-) used for punctuation or pauses. Use commas or periods instead.
   - NO fluff words (e.g., "moreover", "furthermore", "thus").
   - NO AI-sounding introductory fluff (e.g., "I hope this email finds you well").
   - DO NOT write "see resume with deeper breakdowns of these metrics" or similar filler referring to the resume.
   - Never write "Please find attached."

## Email Structure (FOLLOW THIS STRICTLY EVERY TIME)

You must structure EVERY email into exactly these sections:

1. **Greeting:** Hey [First name], 
2. **Where I Saw The Job:** 1 sentence explicitly stating where you saw the job/opening.
3. **Intro & Suitability:** The STRICT intro sentence.
4. **Relevance Match:** A concise sentence expressing excitement because your background aligns perfectly with the JD.
5. **Systems & Automation (Paragraph 2):** Conversational storytelling connecting your experience to the company's challenge using core truths.
6. **Portfolio Link:** Include the portfolio URL naturally in the text.
7. **Action Note:** A call to action (e.g., Let's schedule a call soon to discuss how I can help.)
8. **Looking Forward:** "Looking forward to talking to you guys."
9. **Sign-off:** "Thanks and regards," followed by signature block.
